About Me

Among the several reasons that led me to pursue a career in a UX design, a few are very important to me. I’ve always been curious about what motivates people to do what they do but over the last couple of years, my curiosity has developed into a passion. Reading books like ‘Thinking Fast and Slow’ by Daniel Kahneman, ‘Hooked’ by Nir Eyal, ‘Principles’ by Ray Dalio have made an impact but I’ve always found user research interesting, just that I didn’t know it.

During my previous career in media analytics, I used to eagerly wait for the weekly television rating reports to find out how viewers are responding to the various network shows. My favorite part of the job was digging deeper into those reports and uncovering valuable insights to generate an action plan. This was clearly the most exciting part of my job because I wanted to learn and define the cause and effect so that we could try and improve. Though at a small scale, I successfully initiated few qualitative research projects, in an industry that’s heavily guided by third party research data, just out of my curiosity and it turned out to be very productive.

So when I was introduced to the field of UX design, I was fascinated to learn about the user-centric approach applied in problem-solving. The clear emphasis on understanding the user, identifying the root cause of a problem before jumping in to try and solve it, really resonated with me. I’ve always believed in the power of research and data analytics and now have created an opportunity to have a job where I will enjoy what I do.

Last but not the least a career in UX design will let me be creative and analytical at the same time keeping both sides of my brain busy :)
