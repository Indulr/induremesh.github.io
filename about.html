About Me

Among all the reasons for why I chose UX design, few are very important to me. I have always been curious about what motivates people to do what they do. But in recent times my curiosity has turned into a passion especially after reading books like Thinking fast and slow by Daniel Kahneman, Hooked by Nir Eyal, etc. When I was introduced to the field of UX design, I was fascinated by the user-centered approach in problem-solving. The emphasis on understanding the user and finding the root cause of a problem without jumping in trying to solve it really resonated with me because I have always been a believer in the power of research and data.

During my previous career in media analytics, I used to eagerly wait for weekly television rating reports to find out how people are responding to various shows. My favorite part of the job was digging deeper into those reports and uncovering valuable insights to make an action plan and this process got me excited every time.
